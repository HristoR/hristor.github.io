Crow's Nest presented a unique challenge: balancing production leadership responsibilities with hands-on systems design contributions. This dual-role position required not only coordinating a 13-person cross-disciplinary team but also contributing directly to game design while demonstrating how technical understanding enhances production capabilities.

The Challenge

Leading production for a 13-person team developing a cooperative ship management game in Unreal Engine 5. The project required coordinating across art, design, and programming disciplines while managing a complex asset pipeline and ensuring milestone deliveries remained on schedule.

Production Responsibilities

  • Managed complex UE5 asset pipeline across departments
  • Implemented Perforce version control with custom workflows
  • Coordinated daily standups for 13 members
  • Facilitated cross-specialization communication
  • Tracked risks and dependencies through sprint cycles

Design Contributions

Ship 3Cs & Controls

Created and iterated on the ship's core movement and control systems. Led multiple playtest sessions and implemented feedback to refine ship navigation, working closely with programmers to balance responsiveness with cooperative gameplay focus.

Production Insight: Owning the 3Cs design gave direct insight into technical constraints, enabling better production planning and more accurate sprint estimates for gameplay features.

Systems Documentation

  • Created comprehensive system design documentation for core mechanics
  • Developed feature specifications used by programming team
  • Designed feedback systems for ship damage and player actions
  • Prototyped station interactions and cooperative gameplay loops

Production Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Initial Pitch Rejection & Morale Recovery

The team's initial pitch was declined after two days of passionate work, leading to significant morale drop and uncertainty about how to proceed.

Solution

Maintained team confidence through structured recovery. Facilitated guided concepting sessions to iterate on the design, incorporated stakeholder feedback constructively, and successfully re-pitched within the same week. This taught the team resilience and the importance of iteration.

Challenge: Balancing Producer and Designer Responsibilities

Early in the project, took on too much production lead role while neglecting growth as a designer, as identified through mentor feedback.

Solution

After mentor feedback, consciously stepped back from some production meetings to focus on design work. Structured time to ensure both production coordination and design contributions received proper attention, ultimately making me more effective in both roles.

Challenge: Complex Asset Pipeline Management

Coordinating asset delivery and integration across 13 team members in Unreal Engine 5 with Perforce required careful dependency tracking and conflict prevention.

Solution

Implemented custom Perforce workflows tailored to team structure. Created clear documentation for asset submission and review processes. Established "integration windows" where artists and programmers coordinated asset handoffs, significantly reducing merge conflicts.

Key Learnings & Growth

  • Resilience in Production: Learned that setbacks like pitch rejections are opportunities for improvement. The ability to recover and rally the team is as important as initial planning.
  • Dual-Role Benefits: Understanding both production and design perspectives made me more effective at both. Technical knowledge directly improved production planning accuracy.
  • Prioritization Under Pressure: Developed the critical skill of knowing when to close feature development and focus on polish.
  • Dependency Visualization: Creating dependency maps transformed abstract risks into concrete, visible blockers the entire team could understand.
  • Team Atmosphere Matters: Building an environment where team members felt appreciated significantly improved productivity and morale.
